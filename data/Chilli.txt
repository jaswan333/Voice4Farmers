Chilli - Cercospora Leaf Spot (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are brown spots forming on leaves before they dry?

Why are leaves showing many small round patches?

Symptoms:

Small round spots with dark brown margin and gray or pale center

Spots may join, leaves turn yellow and drop early

Reduced leaves, poor flowering and lower yield

Cause:
Fungal disease caused by Cercospora capsici in humid, wet weather.

Solution:

Spray Mancozeb or Chlorothalonil 0.2–0.3% at first spots, repeat 2–3 times at 10‑day intervals

Avoid spraying during peak sunshine; follow label and safety interval

Prevention:

Keep spacing open for good air movement

Avoid overhead irrigation on leaves

Remove infected leaves and burn crop residues after harvest

Chilli - Chilli Wilt (Fusarium / Bacterial Wilt) (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are plants suddenly wilting even though soil is wet?

Why is one stem browning inside while leaves stay green?

Symptoms:

One or more branches wilt first, then whole plant collapses

When stem cut, inside shows brown streaks or rot

Roots may be soft and black in severe cases

Cause:
Soil‑borne fungi Fusarium or bacteria Ralstonia entering through roots.

Solution:

Remove and destroy infected plants with surrounding soil

Use disease‑free seedlings and treat them with appropriate fungicides or bio‑agents

Avoid planting chilli again in that patch for at least one season

Prevention:

Rotate with non‑solanaceous crops like cereals or pulses

Avoid heavy, water‑logged soil; use raised beds if needed

Use clean tools and avoid moving soil from infected fields

Chilli - Chilli Leaf Curl Virus (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves curling upward and plants short?

Why are plants bushy with very few fruits?

Symptoms:

Leaves curl upward, become narrow, twisted and pale or yellow‑mottled

Plants short, bushy, with reduced branching and flowering

Fruits small, misshapen, poor colour and low marketability

Cause:
Virus spread by whiteflies that feed on leaves.

Solution:

Remove and destroy infected plants early in the field

Use leaf‑curl‑resistant or tolerant varieties where available

Control whiteflies with Imidacloprid, Acetamiprid or similar sprays at recommended dose

Prevention:

Use virus‑free, certified seedlings from trusted nurseries

Control whiteflies early before populations build up

Remove nearby solanaceous weeds like Datura and Sida

Chilli - Chilli Mosaic / Vein Mottle Virus (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves showing light and dark green patches in lines?

Why are leaves mottled and plants looking weak?

Symptoms:

Leaves with mosaic‑like light and dark green or yellow mottling along veins

Leaves may be bumpy, distorted, plants stunted

Fruits smaller, misshapen, with patchy colour

Cause:
Virus diseases like Cucumber mosaic virus or vein‑mottle‑type viruses, spread by aphids and tools.

Solution:

Remove infected plants early before they spread virus

Use mosaic‑tolerant varieties where available

Control aphids with systemic insecticides like Imidacloprid

Prevention:

Use clean, disease‑free seedlings and disinfect tools

Avoid touching plants with contaminated hands or clothes

Remove nearby weed hosts that shelter aphids

Chilli - Damping‑off (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are seedlings collapsing at the base in nursery?

Why are small plants falling and rotting near soil?

Symptoms:

Seedlings fall at the base, stem near soil water‑soaked, black or brown

Patches of dead, lying seedlings in nursery bed

Poor stand and weak, uneven plants

Cause:
Soil‑borne fungal disease in wet, overcrowded nursery beds.

Solution:

Treat seeds with fungicides or Trichoderma before sowing

Keep nursery beds raised and well‑drained, avoid heavy watering

If disease appears, give light soil drench as per local recommendation

Prevention:

Use clean, disease‑free soil and sterilized trays

Avoid overcrowding seedlings in nursery

Water lightly and frequently, not in standing water

Chilli - Nitrogen Deficiency (Nutrient Deficiency)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are older leaves turning pale yellow?

Why is my chilli crop thin and weak?

Symptoms:

Older leaves pale green to yellow, starting from tip and margin

Plants thin, with fewer branches and slow growth

Fewer flowers, small, light‑coloured fruits

Cause:
Low nitrogen in soil or poor top‑dressing management.

Solution:

Top‑dress with urea 15–25 kg/ha in split doses at vegetative and early flowering stages

Combine with organic manure or compost to improve soil health

Prevention:

Follow balanced NPK recommendations for chilli

Avoid very late, heavy nitrogen doses after flowering

Use responsive, high‑yielding varieties only with good nutrient management

Chilli - Calcium Deficiency – Blossom End Disorder (Nutrient Deficiency)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are fruits rotting at the tip or blossom end?

Why are fruits showing dark, sunken spots at the tip?

Symptoms:

Small, dark, sunken spot at fruit tip or blossom end

Spot may enlarge, fruit quality poor or unmarketable

Plant may look healthy, but many fruits show this tip‑rot

Cause:
Low calcium or uneven water supply during fruit‑setting and bulking.

Solution:

Spray foliar calcium 0.3–0.5% 2–3 times at 7–10‑day intervals during fruit‑setting

Maintain even irrigation with drip or regular light watering

Prevention:

Avoid dry‑wet shock, especially in hot weather

Avoid excess nitrogen that reduces calcium uptake

Use balanced fertilizers with micro‑nutrients on poor soils

Chilli - Thrips (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves getting silvery streaks and tips dried?

Why are flowers dropping and fruits rough or marbled?

Symptoms:

Leaves silvered, with small scratches or streaks from feeding

Leaf tips and margins brown, plants may look burnt

Fruits small, rough, with marbled or uneven colour

Cause:
Thrips feeding on leaves and flowers in hot, dry weather.

Solution:

Spray Spinosad, Imidacloprid, or Thiamethoxam as per label dose at early signs

Spray early morning or late evening for better coverage and safety

Prevention:

Avoid dense, overcrowded planting and tall, soft growth

Reduce high nitrogen, keep canopy airy

Use blue or yellow sticky traps to monitor thrips population

Chilli - Aphids (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves sticky and covered with black sooty mould?

Why are plants yellowing and growth weak?

Symptoms:

Small, soft insects clustered on leaf undersides and tender shoots

Leaves sticky from honeydew, with black sooty mould on top

Plants may be stunted, with smaller leaves and fewer fruits

Cause:
Aphids sucking sap and spreading virus diseases like mosaic.

Solution:

Spray Imidacloprid or Acetamiprid as per label dose at first appearance

Repeat 1–2 times at 7–10‑day intervals if heavy infestation

Prevention:

Avoid heavy nitrogen and very dense planting

Maintain field sanitation and remove nearby weeds

Use yellow sticky traps to track aphid levels

Chilli - Whiteflies (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves sticky and plants looking weak?

Why are fruits small and plants turning yellow slowly?

Symptoms:

Tiny white insects fly when leaves are disturbed

Leaves sticky from honeydew, with black sooty mould on surface

Plants look pale, weak, fruits smaller and fewer

Cause:
Whiteflies feeding on leaves and spreading leaf‑curl and mosaic viruses.

Solution:

Spray Imidacloprid, Acetamiprid or similar as per label dose at early colonization

Use yellow sticky traps to reduce adult whiteflies

Prevention:

Rotate with non‑solanaceous crops and avoid continuous chilli

Keep spacing and water management good to reduce stress

Remove early infected plants and nearby solanaceous weed hosts
