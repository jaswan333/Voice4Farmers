Onion - Purple Blotch (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves getting purplish‑brown spots?

Why are leaves drying and plants weak?

Symptoms:

Elongated, purplish‑brown lesions with dark margin on leaves

Lesions may join and leaves dry from tips downward

Reduced leaves, small bulbs, poor yield

Cause:
Fungal disease caused by Alternaria porri in humid, wet weather.

Solution:

Spray Mancozeb or Chlorothalonil 0.2–0.3% at first sign, repeat 2–3 times at 10–14‑day intervals

Use healthy, disease‑free seedlings and avoid continuous onion

Prevention:

Avoid dense planting and overhead irrigation

Keep field well‑drained, avoid waterlogging

Rotate onion with non‑host crops and remove infected leaves

Onion - Downy Mildew (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves turning pale and purple‑grey underneath?

Why are plants dying in patches?

Symptoms:

Pale green to yellow streaks on leaves, later silvery‑purple

Grey‑purple fluffy growth on leaf undersides in cool, wet weather

Leaves may collapse, plants die in patches

Cause:
Fungal disease caused by Peronospora destructor in cool, moist conditions.

Solution:

Use relatively resistant or tolerant varieties

Spray Mancozeb 0.2–0.3% starting 20 days after planting, repeat 2–3 times at 10‑14‑day intervals in wet weather

Prevention:

Avoid dense planting and prolonged leaf wetness

Use drip or shallow furrow irrigation

Remove and burn infected plants and debris

Onion - White Rot (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are plants suddenly wilting and leaves yellowing?

Why are bulbs rotting with white fungus?

Symptoms:

Leaves yellow and wilt, plant may fall over

White fluffy fungus and black hard sclerotia seen on bulb base

Bulb soft, rotten, often in patches

Cause:
Soil‑borne fungal disease caused by Sclerotium cepivorum.

Solution:

Remove and destroy infected plants and surrounding soil

Avoid replanting onion in that plot for 3–4 years

Prevention:

Rotate with non‑host crops like cereals or pulses

Avoid carrying contaminated soil from infected fields

Use disease‑free seedlings and sets

Onion - Fusarium Basal Rot (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are plants yellowing and bulbs rotting at base?

Why are outer scales brown and soft?

Symptoms:

Lower leaves yellow and wilt, plants look stunted

Basal plate and outer bulb scales turn brown, soft, with fungal growth

Roots may rot, bulb may collapse

Cause:
Fungal disease caused by Fusarium oxysporum f. cepae in infected soil or sets.

Solution:

Remove and destroy infected plants early

Treat seed or sets with fungicides or Trichoderma as per local recommendation

Prevention:

Use disease‑free, certified sets or seedlings

Rotate onion with non‑host crops for several seasons

Avoid continuous onion and over‑irrigation

Onion - Bacterial Soft Rot (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are bulbs turning soft and smelly?

Why are bulbs rotting in storage?

Symptoms:

Fleshy scales turn soft, watery, foul‑smelling

Rot spreads from damaged area to whole bulb

Rot may start in field or continue in storage

Cause:
Bacterial decay caused by Erwinia spp. entering through wounds or bruises.

Solution:

Sort and remove soft, bruised bulbs before storage

Cure bulbs well and keep storage dry, well‑ventilated

Avoid heavy mechanical damage during harvest and transport

Prevention:

Handle bulbs carefully during lifting and grading

Cure onions in shade with good air flow for 10–15 days

Avoid storing in damp, humid godowns

Onion - Nitrogen Deficiency (Nutrient Deficiency)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves pale green or yellow?

Why is crop thin and bulbs small?

Symptoms:

Older leaves pale green to yellow, weaker growth

Plants thin, less tillers, short height

Bulbs smaller than expected

Cause:
Low nitrogen in soil or poor top‑dressing practice.

Solution:

Top‑dress with urea 20–30 kg/ha in split doses during early vegetative and bulb‑formation stages

Combine with organic manure or compost

Prevention:

Follow balanced NPK recommendations for onion

Avoid very late or heavy nitrogen top‑dressing

Include manure at planting if soil is poor

Onion - Potassium Deficiency (Nutrient Deficiency)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaf tips and ends drying out?

Why are plants weak and bulbs soft?

Symptoms:

Leaf tips and margins turn brown, drying like burn

Older leaves show yellowing along edges

Plants weak, bulbs may be small and soft

Cause:
Low potassium in soil, especially in sandy or heavily cropped fields.

Solution:

Apply MOP 15–25 kg/ha split with basal or top‑dressing, as per local advice

Use organic manure to improve soil potassium

Prevention:

Use balanced NPK, avoid only nitrogen‑rich practice

Avoid continuous onion without manure or K

Maintain good drainage and avoid waterlogging

Onion - Thrips (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves silvery and tips dried?

Why are plants stunted and yield poor?

Symptoms:

Leaves silvery, with small scratches or streaks

Tips brown and dry, plants may look burnt

Tiny, slender insects on leaf undersides

Cause:
Thrips feeding on leaves and suck sap, common in hot, dry weather.

Solution:

Spray Dimethoate 30 EC or Fipronil/Imidacloprid as per label dose at first sign

Use blue or yellow sticky traps to monitor and reduce adults

Prevention:

Avoid dense planting and excessive nitrogen

Keep canopy airy, use drip irrigation

Encourage natural enemies by reducing harsh sprays

Onion - Aphids (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves sticky and black?

Why are plants weak and bulbs small?

Symptoms:

Small, soft insects clustered on leaf undersides

Leaves sticky from honeydew, with black sooty mould

Plants may be stunted, with reduced leaf area

Cause:
Aphids sucking sap and secreting honeydew, often in mild weather.

Solution:

Spray Imidacloprid or Acetamiprid as per label dose at early colonization

Repeat 1–2 times at 7–10‑day intervals under heavy pressure

Prevention:

Avoid heavy nitrogen and dense planting

Maintain field sanitation and remove weeds

Use yellow sticky traps where available
