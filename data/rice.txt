=== CROP: Rice ===

Rice - Blast (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are there brown spots on leaves and necks?

Why are some panicles cut off and grains empty?

Symptoms:

Brown, spindle‑shaped spots on leaves with gray center and dark brown margin

Neck blast cuts off the panicle, making grains empty or half‑filled

Spots can also appear on nodes and panicles

Cause:
Fungal infection caused by Pyricularia oryzae in humid, wet conditions.

Solution:

Use resistant varieties like Ajay, Arize Tej, or other blast‑tolerant types

Spray Tricyclazole or Propiconazole 0.2–0.3% at onset of symptoms, repeat 2–3 times at 10–14‑day intervals

Prevention:

Avoid excess nitrogen, especially in disease‑prone areas

Maintain proper spacing and drainage, avoid dense planting

Use clean, disease‑free seed and avoid continuous rice in the same field

Rice - Brown Spot (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are there small brown spots on leaves?

Why are leaves drying and yield low?

Symptoms:

Small round brown spots with light brown center and dark border on leaves

Spots may join and leaves turn yellow and dry early

Reduced tillering and unfilled or chaffy grains in severe cases

Cause:
Fungal disease caused by Bipolaris oryzae, common in mineral‑deficient and humid fields.

Solution:

Use healthy, disease‑free seedlings and balanced NPK with potassium and phosphorus

Spray Mancozeb or Carbendazim 0.2–0.3% at early symptoms, repeat 1–2 times at 10‑day intervals

Prevention:

Avoid excess nitrogen, especially in continuous rice

Maintain good drainage, avoid water stagnation

Remove and burn infected crop residues after harvest

Rice - Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB) (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves drying like burnt edges?

Why are leaves turning yellow and crop dying?

Symptoms:

Long, yellow‑green to white streaks along leaf edges, turning brown

Entire leaves may dry up in heavy infection

Stems may show brown stripes, plants look scorched

Cause:
Bacterial infection caused by Xanthomonas oryzae, entering through wounds and water.

Solution:

Use resistant varieties like Arize Dhani or BLB‑tolerant cultivars

Spray copper‑based bactericides (e.g., copper oxychloride) at label dose at early infection

Prevention:

Avoid excess nitrogen and dense planting

Avoid continuous flooding; keep field water at proper level

Remove infected plants and avoid reuse of diseased seed

Rice - Sheath Blight (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are sheaths rotting near water level?

Why are leaves drying and panicles weak?

Symptoms:

Oval, brown or gray lesions on leaf sheaths near the water line

Lesions may spread to leaves and flag leaf, causing drying

White, web‑like fungal growth seen in humid conditions

Cause:
Fungal disease caused by Rhizoctonia solani in dense, nitrogen‑rich rice.

Solution:

Use semi‑dwarf, erect‑type varieties with good canopy structure

Spray Validamycin or Carbendazim at label dose during maximum tillering to early booting

Prevention:

Avoid excess nitrogen and very dense planting

Keep field water and drainage in check, avoid heavy crowding

Rotate rice with non‑host crops where possible

Rice - Rice Tungro Virus (RTV) (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are plants stunted and yellow?

Why are panicles empty and yield very low?

Symptoms:

Plants short, leaves yellow‑orange or pale, with reduced tillers

Panicles small or missing, unfilled grains

Crop looks patchy in field

Cause:
Virus disease spread by green leafhopper (vector insect).

Solution:

Remove and destroy infected plants early

Use tungro‑tolerant varieties where available

Control green leafhopper with approved insecticides as per local dose

Prevention:

Avoid planting in known tungro‑endemic areas without vector control

Use clean, virus‑free seedlings

Rotate crop or avoid continuous rice where disease is severe

Rice - Nitrogen Deficiency (Nutrient Deficiency)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves pale green or yellow?

Why is crop thin and weak?

Symptoms:

Older leaves turn pale green to yellow, starting from tips

Plants look thin, less tillers, short height

Poor panicle size and low grain yield

Cause:
Low nitrogen in soil or poor fertilizer management.

Solution:

Top‑dress with urea 25–40 kg/ha split at tillering and panicle initiation, if soil test is low

Combine with organic manure to improve soil health

Prevention:

Follow soil test‑based NPK recommendations

Avoid delayed or very late nitrogen top‑dressing

Include organic manure at transplanting

Rice - Potassium Deficiency (Nutrient Deficiency)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaf tips drying and brown?

Why are plants weak and kernels shriveled?

Symptoms:

Leaf tips turn brown, drying upward like burn marks

Older leaves show yellowing along margins

Plants weak, lodging, small and shriveled grains

Cause:
Low potassium in soil, especially in sandy or heavily cropped soils.

Solution:

Apply muriate of potash (MOP) at 20–30 kg/ha split with basal or top‑dressing, as per local advisory

Include FYM or compost to improve potassium retention

Prevention:

Use balanced NPK, avoid only nitrogen‑rich practice

Avoid intensive cropping without manure or K replenishment

Maintain proper drainage and avoid waterlogging

Rice - Stem Borer (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are some plants suddenly drying in patches?

Why are panicles empty or white?

Symptoms:

Central shoot dries and dies, forming “deadheart” in early stage

Mature plants show white, empty or half‑filled panicles (“white ear”)

Holes in stem base and galleries inside stem

Cause:
Insect larvae boring into stems, cutting internal feeding channels.

Solution:

Spray monocrotophos 0.06–0.1% or carbaryl / quinalphos at recommended dose at borer appearance

Use light or pheromone traps in fields where available

Prevention:

Avoid continuous rice; rotate with non‑host crops

Remove and burn stubble and excess crop residue

Avoid dense, overly nitrogen‑rich planting

Rice - Brown Plant Hopper (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are plants turning yellow and drying?

Why are leaves brown and crop lodging?

Symptoms:

Leaves turn yellow, then brown, starting from tips and spreading

Plants may dry in patches, “hopper burn”

Hard, shiny black or brown insects seen at base of plants

Cause:
Sucking insect feeding on stems and leaves, often in heavy nitrogen and dense stands.

Solution:

Spray imidacloprid or carbaryl as per local recommendation at early hopper attack

Avoid broad‑spectrum sprays that kill their natural enemies

Prevention:

Use balanced nitrogen, avoid excess top‑dressing

Maintain proper spacing and water management

Encourage frogs and spiders by reducing harsh chemicals
