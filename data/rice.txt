CROP NAME: Rice (Oryza sativa)

GENERAL INFORMATION:
Rice is a major Kharif crop grown in India. It requires warm and humid climate. 
Ideal temperature: 20°C to 35°C.
Ideal rainfall: 100–200 cm annually.
Suitable soil: Clayey or loamy soil with good water retention.

SEED VARIETIES:
- IR64
- ADT 43
- BPT 5204
- CR 1009

NURSERY MANAGEMENT:
Seeds should be treated with fungicide (Carbendazim 2g per kg seed) before sowing.
Nursery duration: 20–25 days.

FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATION:
Basal dose per acre:
- Urea: 50 kg
- DAP: 50 kg
- MOP (Potash): 25 kg

Top dressing:
Apply remaining nitrogen in 2 splits at tillering and panicle initiation stage.

COMMON DISEASES:

1. RICE BLAST:
Symptoms:
- Diamond-shaped lesions with gray center on leaves.
- Leaf drying in severe cases.

Cause:
Fungal infection (Magnaporthe oryzae).

Treatment:
- Spray Tricyclazole 0.6 g per liter of water.
- Avoid excess nitrogen fertilizer.
- Maintain proper spacing.

2. BACTERIAL LEAF BLIGHT:
Symptoms:
- Yellowing of leaf tips.
- Drying from leaf edges.

Treatment:
- Spray Streptocycline 0.1 g per liter.
- Use resistant varieties.

3. BROWN PLANT HOPPER:
Symptoms:
- Hopper burn patches.
- Stunted growth.

Treatment:
- Spray Imidacloprid 0.3 ml per liter.
- Avoid excessive water stagnation.

IRRIGATION MANAGEMENT:
Maintain standing water of 2–5 cm during vegetative stage.
Drain water 10 days before harvest.

HARVESTING:
Harvest when 80–85% grains turn golden yellow.
