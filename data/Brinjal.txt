=== CROP: Brinjal ===

Brinjal - Bacterial Wilt (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are plants suddenly wilting even though soil is wet?

Why is one stem turning brown inside while leaves stay green?

Symptoms:

One or more branches wilt first, then whole plant collapses

When stem is cut, inside is brown and may ooze in water

Roots may be soft and black in severe cases

Cause:
Bacterial disease caused by Ralstonia solanacearum entering through roots or wounds.

Solution:

Remove and destroy infected plants with surrounding soil

Use disease‑free seedlings and treat them with appropriate fungicides or Trichoderma

Avoid planting in that plot immediately for next season

Prevention:

Rotate with non‑solanaceous crops like cereals or pulses

Avoid heavy, water‑logged soil; use raised beds if needed

Keep tools and equipment clean to avoid spreading bacteria

Brinjal - Damping‑off (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are seedlings collapsing at the base in nursery?

Why are small plants falling and rotting near soil?

Symptoms:

Seedlings fall at base, stem near soil water‑soaked, black or brown

Patches of dead, lying seedlings in nursery bed

Poor stand and weak, uneven plants

Cause:
Soil‑borne fungal disease in wet, overcrowded nursery beds.

Solution:

Treat seeds with fungicides or Trichoderma before sowing

Keep nursery beds raised and well‑drained, avoid heavy watering

If disease appears, give light soil drench as per local recommendation

Prevention:

Use clean, disease‑free soil and sterilized trays

Avoid overcrowding seedlings in nursery

Water lightly and frequently, not in standing water

Brinjal - Brinjal Little Leaf (Phytoplasma) (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves very small and pale yellow?

Why are plants bushy, with twisted branches and no normal fruits?

Symptoms:

Leaves small, thin, pale yellow, with distorted shape

Plants bushy, rosette‑like, with many short, stunted branches

Few flowers, most fruits small, hard, and misshapen

Cause:
Phytoplasma disease spread by leafhoppers between plants.

Solution:

Remove and destroy infected plants early

Use little‑leaf‑tolerant varieties where available

Control leafhoppers with approved insecticides

Prevention:

Use healthy, disease‑free seedlings

Control leafhopper vectors early with Imidacloprid or similar sprays

Remove nearby solanaceous weeds that host the vector

Brinjal - Mosaic and Leaf Curl Viruses (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves showing light and dark green patches in patches?

Why are leaves curling and plants looking stunted?

Symptoms:

Leaves with mosaic‑like light and dark green or yellow mottling

Leaves curled, twisted, plants short and weak

Fruits small, misshapovan, poor colour and shape

Cause:
Virus diseases spread by whiteflies or aphids feeding on leaves.

Solution:

Remove infected plants early before viruses spread

Use mosaic or leaf‑curl‑resistant varieties where available

Control whiteflies or aphids with systemic insecticides

Prevention:

Use virus‑free, certified seedlings

Apply early vector control sprays in virus‑prone areas

Remove nearby weeds like Datura and Sida that host vectors

Brinjal - Alternaria / Cercospora Leaf Spot (Disease)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves getting brown or circular patches?

Why are leaves drying from bottom of plant?

Symptoms:

Small round or irregular brown spots on leaves with dark margin

Spots may join, leaves turn yellow and drop early

Reduced leaves, fewer flowers and fruits, lower yield

Cause:
Fungal disease caused by Alternaria or Cercospora in humid, wet conditions.

Solution:

Spray Mancozeb or Chlorothalonil 0.2–0.3% at early spots, repeat 2–3 times at 10‑day intervals

Avoid spraying during peak sunshine

Prevention:

Maintain proper spacing for good air movement

Avoid overhead irrigation on leaves

Remove infected leaves and burn crop residues after harvest

Brinjal - Nutrient Deficiency – Nitrogen (Nutrient Deficiency)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are older leaves turning pale yellow?

Why is crop thin and weak, with few fruits?

Symptoms:

Older leaves pale green to yellow, starting from tip and margin

Plants thin, with fewer branches and slower growth

Fewer flowers and fruits, small, light‑coloured fruits

Cause:
Low nitrogen in soil or poor fertilizer management.

Solution:

Top‑dress with urea 15–25 kg/ha in split doses at vegetative and early flowering stages

Combine with organic manure or compost to improve soil health

Prevention:

Follow balanced NPK recommendations for brinjal

Avoid heavy, late nitrogen after flowering

Use responsive high‑yielding varieties only with good nutrient supply

Brinjal - Nutrient Deficiency – Potassium (Nutrient Deficiency)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaf tips and edges drying out and brown?

Why are plants weak and fruits soft or shrivelled?

Symptoms:

Leaf tips and margins turn brown, dry like burn marks

Older leaves show yellowing along edges

Plants look weak, fruits may be small and soft

Cause:
Low potassium in soil, especially in sandy or heavily cropped fields.

Solution:

Apply MOP 15–25 kg/ha split with basal or top‑dressing, as per local advice

Include organic manure or compost to improve soil K

Prevention:

Use balanced NPK, avoid only nitrogen‑rich practice

Avoid continuous brinjal without K or manure

Keep good drainage and avoid waterlogging

Brinjal - Shoot and Fruit Borer (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are young shoots drying and dropping?

Why are fruits rotting with small holes and black droppings?

Symptoms:

Young shoots and tender fruits show holes and black droppings

Shoots wither and dry from tip downwards

Fruits partially or completely rotten from inside

Cause:
Larvae of shoot and fruit borer boring into shoots and fruits.

Solution:

Spray Carbaryl, Quinalphos, Spinosad or approved insecticides as per label at early signs

Repeat 2–3 times at 10–15‑day intervals in high‑pressure areas

Prevention:

Remove and destroy affected shoots and rotten fruits

Keep spacing open to improve spray coverage

Avoid continuous brinjal in same field

Brinjal - Thrips (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves silvery and tips dried out?

Why are fruits rough, small and marbled?

Symptoms:

Leaves silvery, scratched, with small streaks

Leaf tips and margins brown, plants may look burnt

Fruits small, rough, with marbled or uneven colour

Cause:
Thrips feeding on leaves and fruits in hot, dry weather.

Solution:

Spray Spinosad, Imidacloprid or Thiamethoxam as per label at early signs

Spray early morning or late evening for better coverage

Prevention:

Avoid dense, overcrowded planting

Reduce high nitrogen, keep canopy airy

Use blue or yellow sticky traps to monitor thrips

Brinjal - Aphids and Leafhoppers (Pest)

Common Farmer Questions:

Why are leaves sticky and covered with black sooty mould?

Why are leaves yellowing and stunted?

Symptoms:

Small soft insects on leaf undersides and tender shoots

Leaves sticky from honeydew, with black sooty mould

Plants may be stunted, with reduced growth and fewer fruits

Cause:
Aphids and leafhoppers sucking sap and sometimes spreading viruses.

Solution:

Spray Imidacloprid or Acetamiprid as per label dose at first appearance

Repeat 1–2 times at 7–10‑day intervals under heavy pressure

Prevention:

Avoid heavy nitrogen and dense planting

Maintain field sanitation and remove nearby weeds

Use yellow or blue sticky traps to monitor pests
